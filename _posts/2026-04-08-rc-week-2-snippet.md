## Context about what "Recursers" do day-to-day
As a remote _recurser_, RC provides me with the following structure that helps me stay in the loop:

* Coffee chatbot that pairs people for a quick 1:1s.
* Events for moderated pair programming, which is the key to the retreat. The format is:
  intro, then a few hours of programming and demo at the end.
* Project presentations and demos. These are scheduled via an internal app where folks can sign up for a 5 min
  presentation.

Overall I find this structure really well measured, it's not overwhelming yet provides regular
social accountability with a touch of kindness.

## Log of my progress since the first week
* Completed half of [Ziglings](https://codeberg.org/leov/exercises) - tiny coding problems to learn the basics of Zig.
* The article that prompted my interest in the ["Writing a software calculator problem"](https://asteriskmag.substack.com/p/the-impossible-calculator).
* Android code archeology to find a version of alculator written by [Hans J. Boehm](https://www.hboehm.info/new_crcalc/)
  that I want to rebuild in Zig ([code](https://android.googlesource.com/platform/external/crcalc/+/6db978c639e9bd5ac63fd88cbf3765d8c0fb3271)).
* I got into the rabbit hole of tracing work on calculators and ended up reading this fascinating paper
  from Jon von Neumann et al: ["Preliminary Discussion of the Logical Design of an Electronic Computing Instrument (28 June 1946)"](https://www.ias.edu/sites/default/files/library/Prelim_Disc_Logical_Design.pdf).
* Discovered <https://ziggit.dev> which looks like an actual place where Zig development happens. Stumbled
  on the [blog post by Matklad](https://matklad.github.io/2026/02/11/programming-aphorisms.html),
  The quote - _"meta meta: ... Reading Ziggit is part of trick-acquisition routine for me"_ 

What remains challenging is finding time to communicate outwards via writing / pairing up with
people to discuss ideas.
